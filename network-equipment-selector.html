RFP's — All RFP's must be submitted by October 30th for consideration
A Request for Proposal, or RFP, may be required if the services that are being sought by your organization are not able to be described through the limited abilities of a Form 470. An RFP must be accompanied by a Form 470 that is filed and references the RFP in the EPC system. In cases where an RFP is necessary, the RFP is not considered to be complete until its corresponding Form 470 is released, and the Form 470 is not considered to be complete until the corresponding RFP is released. An RFP must also abide by all competitive bidding rules that apply to a Form 470. An RFP allows for you to provide more information regarding project specifications, timelines, requirements, contract conditions, diagrams, layouts, and response requirements, to ensure that a vendor's proposal in response to your service request meet your requirements.
When might an RFP be required for E-Rate eligible services? Building infrastructure projects for new cable plant typically require an RFP. The provision and installation of equipment throughout your building, for example, access point placements. Equipping of a newly constructed building. Requests for dark or self-provisioned fiber that connects two or more entities not on a single contiguous campus. Complex multi-year contracts for service.
